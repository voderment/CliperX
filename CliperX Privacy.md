Privacy Policy for CliperX

Last updated: 2026-05-14

CliperX is a clipboard manager designed for the Apple ecosystem, including Mac, iPhone, and iPad. We design the app to keep your clipboard history and saved content under your control and to minimize data collection.

Features may vary by platform. On Mac, CliperX can automatically save clipboard history while the app is running. On iPhone and iPad, CliperX works through iOS-supported, user-initiated workflows such as saving content from the app, using the share sheet, copying saved items again, and accessing saved items from the keyboard extension where available.

1) Information We Process

- Clipboard history, saved content, and payloads: text, links, images, files, rich text, code, symbols, and other supported formats needed to provide history, preview, search, sync, copy, and paste-back features.
- Item metadata: timestamps, content type, source app name/bundle identifier when available, custom group or mark assignment, favorite/trash state, and display metadata such as summary text, preview metadata, symbols, or source app icons.
- Settings: preferences such as history limit, retention period, language/appearance, panel behavior, sound effects, screen-sharing visibility, tap/copy behavior, keyboard behavior, and paste behavior options.
- Mac clipboard and paste actions:
  - On Mac, CliperX may monitor the system pasteboard while running to save clipboard history according to your settings.
  - When you choose "Copy to Clipboard" or "Paste into Active App", CliperX stores the destination preference and applies selected items according to that choice.
- iOS and iPadOS user-initiated actions:
  - When you save, share, copy, or reuse content through CliperX, the app processes the content and metadata needed for that action.
  - The share extension receives only the content you explicitly share to CliperX through the system share sheet.
  - The keyboard extension may read a compact local index of saved items so you can access selected content while typing.
  - CliperX does not continuously read the clipboard in the background on iOS or iPadOS.
  - The keyboard extension does not record what you type.
- Permission-dependent processing:
  - Accessibility permission on Mac is used only for "Paste into Active App".
  - The app inserts user-selected clipboard content into the currently focused input field only when you explicitly trigger the action.
  - The app does not monitor your keystrokes, does not read or collect unrelated text content from other applications, and does not record what you type.
- Optional iCloud sync data: if iCloud sync is enabled, sync records are stored in your Apple CloudKit private database to keep your data consistent across your own devices.
- Optional backup/import data: when you export a backup, CliperX packages your local app data into a backup file; when you import, CliperX reads the selected backup file to restore data.
- URL and rich-preview network data: for link and media previews, CliperX may request webpage metadata, Open Graph metadata, favicon resources, or Apple media lookup metadata needed to show previews.

2) How We Use Information

- Provide core product functions: capture or save content, organize items, preview content, search history, sync data, copy items again, and reuse saved content.
- Provide Mac insertion workflows, including "Paste into Active App", based on your settings and granted permissions.
- Provide iOS and iPadOS save and reuse workflows, including share extension and keyboard extension access where available.
- Keep your settings and data available between launches.
- Sync your data between your devices when iCloud sync is enabled.
- Import/export backups only when you initiate those actions.
- Perform user-triggered copy or paste actions without accessing or collecting unrelated application data.

3) Where Data Is Stored

- Local storage: app data is primarily stored locally on your device, such as in the app's Application Support storage or the platform's app container.
- Shared app-group storage: on iOS and iPadOS, compact shared data may be stored in the local app group so extensions such as the keyboard or share extension can access the saved-item information they need.
- Permission status and feature preferences are stored locally to keep behavior consistent.
- iCloud storage (optional): when enabled, sync data is stored in your CloudKit private database associated with your Apple ID.
- Backup destination (user-chosen): exported backup files are written only to the location you select.

4) Data Sharing and Third Parties

- We do not sell your clipboard data.
- We do not use advertising SDKs.
- We do not share clipboard content with third-party analytics platforms.
- Third-party processing may occur only as part of requested functionality:
  - Apple services such as CloudKit, APNs, iCloud, and system extension frameworks for sync, push delivery, storage, and platform workflows.
  - Website, metadata, media lookup, and favicon providers for link and rich-preview resources.

5) Retention and Deletion

- Retention follows your in-app settings, including maximum saved items and retention duration where available.
- You can delete individual entries, clear history, remove synced items, or manage saved content in the app.
- Data in blocked source apps on Mac is not newly captured while those apps remain blocked.
- If iCloud sync is enabled, deletion changes may sync across your devices.
- Exported backup files remain wherever you saved them until you delete them.

6) Your Choices and Controls

You can, at any time:

- Enable, disable, or stop using clipboard history and save/reuse features provided by the app.
- Configure source-app privacy blocklists on supported platforms.
- Adjust retention policies and maximum history size where available.
- Choose how selected items are applied, such as Copy to Clipboard or Paste into Active App on supported platforms.
- Control whether app UI is visible during screen sharing on supported platforms.
- Grant, deny, or revoke Accessibility permission in macOS System Settings.
- If Accessibility permission is revoked, "Paste into Active App" on Mac will no longer be available until permission is granted again.
- Enable, disable, or stop using the iOS share extension or keyboard extension through system settings and app workflows.
- Use or stop using iCloud sync.
- Create, keep, or delete exported backups.

7) Security

We apply reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect data handled by the app. However, no method of storage or transmission is guaranteed to be 100% secure.

8) Children's Privacy

CliperX is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9) Policy Changes

We may update this policy as features or legal requirements change. The "Last updated" date will reflect the latest revision.
